Evera creates dialogical reconstructions of historical figures. We disclose how the system works and where the ethical boundaries lie.

1. Who the “Eternals” are

The “Eternals” are digital personas built from vetted sources: texts, letters, lectures, and contemporary accounts. They are not digital twins of living people nor claims to absolute historical truth. Their purpose is to reveal a thinker’s worldview and inspire further study.

2. Data provenance

Each dialogue is grounded in publicly available or licensed materials. Sources undergo both manual and automated verification, and references are logged in Evera’s archive. We do not use restricted datasets without permission, nor do we appropriate work from modern authors without consent.

3. Role of neural networks

Evera employs a hybrid system: a structured knowledge base, linguistic models, and authored scripts. The model never answers autonomously — every dialogue is guided by Evera methodology and checked against source material.

4. Ethical safeguards

  • Requests that demean historical figures or incite hatred are disallowed.
  • Digital personas do not provide legal, medical, or financial advice.
  • Dialogues are not used for political campaigning or manipulation.

5. Limitation of liability

Responses from the Eternals are reconstructions, not direct quotations. Evera is not responsible for interpretations made by users. Verify with original sources before citing the material.

6. Model updates

Algorithms and scripts are updated regularly. Changes are recorded in the Evera log and accompanied by renewed ethical and source review.

7. Feedback

If you encounter an error, inaccuracy, or ethical concern, contact: @solomonidis. Every report is reviewed and the system adjusted as needed.
